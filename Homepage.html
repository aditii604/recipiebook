Cooking is a broad term that refers to the preparation of food for consumption, and it can involve many different methods, techniques, and ingredients: Cooking methods There are many different cooking methods, including moist-heat and dry-heat cooking. Moist-heat cooking methods include boiling, poaching, simmering, and steaming. Dry-heat cooking methods include baking, roasting, frying, grilling, barbecuing, and smoking.

Cooking techniques
The method used to cook food can significantly affect the final result. For example, short cooking methods like stir-frying and blanching can help reduce the loss of vitamins, while longer cooking methods like roasting can cause more vitamin degradation.
Cooking ingredients
The ingredients used to make food can vary widely, and the vitamin content of food can depend on whether it contains fat-soluble or water-soluble vitamins. Fat-soluble vitamins are found in foods like dairy products, vegetable oils, and oily fish, while water-soluble vitamins are found in foods like fruits and vegetables.
Cooking as a tradition
Cooking is an important practice in many cultures and traditions, and it can be seen as a way to express connection and happiness.
Cooking history
The history of cooking can shed light on the origins of human settlement, and its variety and traditions reflect unique social, cultural, and environmental influences.
